Turn your voice into a permanently archived national record

"Voice" becomes a "publication," subject to permanent preservation under national law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quartz glass (silicon dioxide) is one of the most stable substances on Earth. Joint research by Hitachi and Kyoto University demonstrated that laser recording on quartz glass can preserve data for "over 300 million years." 1,000 years is a theoretical value based on the material's scientific properties. QR codes have over 30 years of proven reliability since their development in 1994 and are backed by ISO international standards ensuring backward compatibility.

TokiStorage's greatest strength is being a physical medium that requires no server, no app, and no electricity. Records engraved in quartz glass continue to exist regardless of our company's business continuity. Three-layer distributed storage (physical, National Diet Library, GitHub) ensures records are preserved through multiple independent systems.

Before the production process begins, you receive a full refund — no questions asked. After production starts, a refund minus actual costs incurred is provided. We believe that the ability to leave at any time is the foundation of trust.

It's natural to feel cautious. "1,000 years," "islands," "emotional story" — these overlap with scam patterns. We've addressed every one of these concerns openly. Please read "Before You Decide, Question Everything" — our transparency page where we list every reason this might look suspicious and answer each one. Founder identity, scientific evidence, refund policy, and third-party verification resources are all published.

In Japan, a corporation must pay roughly ¥70,000/year in resident tax even with zero revenue, plus mandatory annual tax filings, accountant fees, and social insurance obligations. Operating a service designed to be free from time pressure inside a structure that demands annual costs would be a structural contradiction. As a sole proprietor, maintenance cost is near zero whether dormant for one year or ten. The moment we resume, the blue-return tax filing (¥650,000 deduction) is ready and waiting. Just as quartz glass endures without electricity or servers, we chose a business structure that loses nothing by standing still. Incorporation becomes the right move when stable revenue makes the tax benefits outweigh the carrying costs — and there's no rush to reach that point either.
