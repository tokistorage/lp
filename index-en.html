"Voice" becomes a "publication," subject to permanent preservation under national law.

Quartz glass (silicon dioxide) is one of the most stable substances on Earth. Joint research by Hitachi and Kyoto University demonstrated that laser recording on quartz glass can preserve data for "over 300 million years." 1,000 years is a theoretical value based on the material's scientific properties. QR codes have over 30 years of proven reliability since their development in 1994 and are backed by ISO international standards ensuring backward compatibility.
Read about the journey from shells and leaves to quartz glass here.

Three-layer distributed storage (physical, National Diet Library, GitHub) ensures records are preserved through multiple independent systems.

In Japan, a corporation must pay roughly ¥70,000/year in resident tax even with zero revenue, plus mandatory annual tax filings, accountant fees, and social insurance obligations. Just as quartz glass endures without electricity or servers, we chose a business structure that loses nothing by standing still. Incorporation becomes the right move when stable revenue makes the tax benefits outweigh the carrying costs — and there's no rush to reach that point either.
