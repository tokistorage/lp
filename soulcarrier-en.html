Pearl’s Story (2007–2025)

Pearl was our family dog for 18 years. In her final years, she lost her voice. We preserved her throat bone with our daughter’s four-leaf clover and flower petals in resin, nestled inside a clamshell—a portable grave. This experience led to voice-encoded QR technology, and ultimately to SoulCarrier.

Moving between Maui and Japan, we met people standing at boundaries—outside systems, disconnected from their roots, carrying burdens alone for years. “Cross boundaries through resonance”—the principle Pearl taught us became SoulCarrier’s mission.

Why existing services fall short

Language barrier

Government offices and temples have limited English support. Obtaining family registries, negotiating with head priests, contacting cemetery managers—everything requires Japanese.

Institutional barrier

Without a registered address in Japan, procedures become extremely difficult. Registry requests and reburial permits assume you’re a resident.

Time barrier

No one handles everything from research to accompaniment. Hiring a legal scrivener, interpreter, and travel agent separately costs time and money.

Emotional barrier

No one approaches this as “completing a family story” rather than “processing paperwork.” Gathering documents and delivering a family’s narrative are different things.
