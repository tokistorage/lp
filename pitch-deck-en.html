02 - Problem

Different Time Horizons, Different Design

Digital and stone each excel at their own timescale. The millennium layer is missing.

💾

Digital: Optimized for Today

Cloud and social platforms are superb at everyday recording and sharing. But S&P 500 companies average under 20 years of lifespan (McKinsey). Preservation at 100- or 1,000-year timescales demands a different design philosophy.

🪦

Gravestones: Built for Reverence

Over 100,000 graves are decommissioned annually in Japan alone. Headstones excel as places to gather and pay respects, but last 100–200 years. Declining birthrates and urbanization create demand for a complementary preservation layer.

👤

The Inequality of Memory

Only the privileged few leave records in history. An ordinary person's existence vanishes within 3 generations. "Can you name your great-grandparents?"

Structural Market Opportunity: Digital services excel at "everyday records." Gravestones excel at "the reverence experience." But no individual-facing layer exists for "millennium-scale information preservation." The opportunity lies not in replacing existing services, but in complementing them with a new, enduring layer.
