ü¶ñ Why Are Dinosaur Fossils Important?

Dinosaurs went extinct about 66 million years ago. But we still know about them. How?

Because of fossils. Their bones and footprints turned into stone and were preserved. That's why we can learn about creatures from millions of years ago.

üí° Fun Fact

If there were no fossils, no one would even know dinosaurs existed. Things that "remain" help us "know."

üì± Will Your Phone Photos Last?

You probably have tons of photos on your phone or tablet. But will they really last 1000 years?

Actually, digital data is surprisingly easy to lose.

  • What if your phone breaks?
  • What if the cloud company shuts down?
  • What if the file format becomes unreadable?

Photos from 100 years ago (on paper) can still be seen today. But floppy disks from just 20 years ago? Most computers can't read them anymore.

üí° By the Way

Data carved into quartz glass can theoretically last 300 million years!

üéÅ What Does "Leaving Something Behind" Mean?

"Leaving something behind" is like a gift to the future.

The person who receives it can't say thank you. Because it arrives after you're gone.

But why would someone still want to leave something?

Maybe because they want someone to know "I was here." They want to connect their thoughts and feelings to the future.
